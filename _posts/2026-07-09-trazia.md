Trazia is a free coloring-page site I built. Type a sentence — "a little dinosaur in a hat having a picnic" — and it generates a clean black-and-white line drawing. Or drop in a photo, and it turns that into a printable outline right in your browser. Good for keeping kids busy, making classroom materials, or just relaxing with a coloring page as an adult.

> 💡 Visit: <https://trazia.art>
{: .prompt-tip }

## What you get

**① Text → coloring page**

- Describe what you want; the AI draws the line art (powered by Cloudflare Workers AI `flux-1-schnell`)
- **Three difficulty levels**: toddler (ages 2–4, extra-thick outlines, big shapes), kids (ages 5–9), and advanced (detailed, for adults)
- The result is binarized in the browser into clean pure-black lines — no grey noise

**② Photo → coloring page**

- Upload a photo; local edge detection extracts the outline — **nothing is uploaded, instant, private**
- Great for turning pets, family portraits, or trip photos into a personal coloring page

**③ Print-ready**

- Export a **PDF sized for A4 / US-Letter**, or save a PNG, or print directly
- The layout is already set — it comes out as a ready-to-color sheet, no margin fiddling

## A note on the build

Trazia isn't just a generator — it's also a **programmatic-SEO content site**: 13 languages and 500+ static pages so far, each theme on a native slug (`/de/tiere/hund`, `/fr/animaux/licorne`) with `hreflang`.

I deliberately skipped the English red ocean — that market is already saturated by sites like ColorBliss. Instead I'm going after the **soft underbelly of non-English markets**, starting with German: `ausmalbilder` ("coloring pages" in German) has ~100k monthly searches with low competition, and the English players mostly ignore it. That's what this site is really testing: native-language content plus a free tool, aimed at the language markets everyone overlooks.

## Some limits

- **Completely free, no signup** to generate and print
- **Original themes only**: requests for Pokémon / Disney / Labubu and other IP are refused outright (HTTP 422) — only non-infringing, original characters
- Generation runs on a free tier, so it may queue under load — deliberate, to keep it free
